Student Handbook

General

Community

This is probably the most important set of rules we have. Our community is what makes us successful as a company, and what makes you successful as students. This program is less about memorizing algorithms and much more about learning how, and who, to ask questions.

This is more than a set of rules to follow while you're here. This is meant to be a guide in how to deal with other developers, other students in the "real world", and to remind you that you will always be a student. Programming means never giving up learning, never stopping growth.

Internal

  • Ask questions
  • Answer questions
  • Speak up
  • Correct people when they make conceptual mistakes
    • But not when they make minor errors.
    • Make sure your correction actually helps someone learn, instead of showing off what you know.
  • Share knowledge. If you learn something, teach it to someone else. This helps you learn it better.
  • Say "I don't know" at least once a day.
  • Solve your own "I don't know", at least once a day.

External

Joining Hackbright means joining a community of programmers who help each other. That community intersects with the outside world, and here are the things you should do to help yourself, and help your community thrive.

  • Make a twitter handle

  • Signup on Github

  • Start a blog

  • Join ##HackbrightAcademy on Freenode (IRC)

Housekeeping

Kitchen

Follow these rules so that our staff doesn't kill itself, and our space stays nice.

  • If you use the kitchen, or dishes, clean up after yourself. Don't leave things in the sink for other people to clean.
  • Things not labeled in the fridge are Free Food. Please eat it.
  • Things without a date on them in the fridge will be thrown out on Fridays. Things with a date will be thrown out if they are older than 5 days old.
  • We don't have a cleaning staff. If you leave a mess, you're asking students and staff to clean it up for you.
  • The small fridge is for HB staff only.

Pairing Stations

The pairing stations are shared computers to be worked on, use these guidelines to use them

  • All drinks must have lids.
  • We don't have a cleaning staff. Don't leave trash around.
  • Please keep your nametag next to you for the first 3 weeks.
  • These computers should be considered "public", so don't leave accounts logged in overnight.
  • Commit and push to github frequently. We don't guarantee the computers won't die, because that's how machines work. This is a great habit to get into.
  • Don't get distracted.

Space / Security

These are the rules for the space - if you're physically here, it doesn't matter if you're a Hackbright student or not. These rules are probably the only ones that can get you kicked out for not following.

  • If you damage something, you are responsible for fixing or replacing the thing that was damaged.
  • If you don't have a keyfob, you need to sign in on the signin board.
  • If you DO have a keyfob, you need to be introduced to the current class of HB students if you haven't been, before you can hang out.
  • Don't leave trash around, please respect our space.
  • Don't torrent on our connection, our routers can't handle it. Also, please don't set up a Tor exit point on our connection.
  • We're an engineering school, please treat our network as "hostile". Students are apt to "experiment" with "security techniques". We aren't responsible for anything that happens to your system on our network, but please ask us if you need help securing your system.
  • If you are an experienced engineer, please answer questions if asked, and be encouraging. Any denigration of someone is grounds to be asked to leave.
  • If you want to have a visitor, they need to be signed in. You are responsible for all visitors that you've brought in.
  • If you answer the door, don't let anyone in that you don't recognize if you don't feel comfortable doing so. If someone says they are the visitor of another person, Have them wait outside until that person comes downstairs to collect them.

Yourself

Health

These are things people tend to forget about during Hackbright, and we mean them very seriously. You can't be a great programmer without taking care of yourself.

  • Sleep. Seriously, you can't not sleep.
  • Eat. Real food - make yourself something, or eat some Free Food from the fridge.
  • Drink Water. Don't drink soda, don't drink tea or coffee until you've had at least one cup of water. Keep track of it somewhere. Get half your body weight(in lbs) in ounces, per day.
  • Caffeine is your friend, but it's not liquid sleep. Seriously, go to sleep.
  • If you're sick, don't come in. Be actually sick though, not just overwhelmed.
  • If you're overwhelmed, talk to us. We are experts at helping with this feeling, and we want you to get through this.

Time

Here's how to best use your time here.

  • Sleep at night.
  • Instructors are here from 10AM to roughly 8PM. If you need help, work with us during that time. Naps during the day might be helpful to you, but you have limited, and expensive, time to get seriously productive 1-on-1 help.
  • Pick a project you enjoy working on. Something you'd like to see done, or something you've always wanted to understand. Don't worry if it's useful, or interesting to anyone but you. This is a time to explore what you do and don't like about programming.
  • Use your mentor. They've signed up to help someone, and they get benefits by just affiliating themselves with Hackbright. Use their skills and knowledge to understand things.
  • Understand your editor. Vim can be really useful, but so can Sublime Text and other IDEs. Pick a tool, then get good at that tool.
  • Cheatsheets are something you're going to want to create. No one expects you to memorize anything, and you're wasting time by trying.
  • If you get stuck, ask for help. If you aren't making progress because you don't understand, then ask.
  • Don't copy-paste and walk away. Programming isn't fire and forget - you need to understand what's going on. If you complete an exercise and you can't explain how you did it, find an instructor.
